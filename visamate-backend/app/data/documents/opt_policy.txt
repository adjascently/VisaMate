OPT (Optional Practical Training) is temporary employment authorization for F-1 students. 
Students may apply for 12 months of post-completion OPT per higher degree level. 
A job offer is NOT required to apply. 
USCIS requires filing Form I-765 and receiving the EAD card before work can begin.

Unemployment Rules:
Students on post-completion OPT may not exceed **90 days of unemployment**. 
Unemployment is counted from the OPT start date printed on the EAD. 
Any day without qualifying employment counts toward this limit.

Types of qualifying employment:
• Paid employment
• Multiple short-term gigs
• Contract work through an agency
• Unpaid volunteer work related to degree (at least 20 hrs/week)
• Self-employment (if business is active + compliant)

Travel while OPT is pending:
Travel is risky because re-entry requires an EAD.
Students should wait for EAD approval before travel.

Documents needed to re-enter on OPT:
• Valid EAD card
• Valid F-1 visa
• Valid passport
• I-20 signed for travel within 6 months
• Proof of employment or job offer
